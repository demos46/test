Demo Page of "MUFFIN: Multi-band Frequency Reconstruction for Neural Psychoacoustic Coding"

--------------------------------> Abstract <---------------------------------


Achieving high-fidelity audio compression is a core challenge in neural audio coding, especially for preserving perceptual quality across diverse audio types. In this paper, we introduce MUFFIN, a novel neural audio codec (NAC) that leverages multi-band spectral decomposition for efficient audio compression and reconstruction. MUFFIN employs multi-band spectral residual vector quantization (MBS-RVQ) to partition and quantize distinct frequency bands, aligning with psychoacoustic principles to enhance perceptual fidelity while optimizing bitrate allocation. We further introduce a high-compression MUFFIN variant achieving a 12.5 kHz compression rate while preserving high audio fidelity. Experimental results on diverse datasets, including LibriTTS, EARS, Music4All, ESC-50, GTZAN, and BBC Sound Effects (totaling over 1,600 hours), demonstrate MUFFINâ€™s state-of-the-art performance in audio reconstruction across varied domains, effectively improving the trade-off between compression rate and reconstruction quality in NAC systems.
